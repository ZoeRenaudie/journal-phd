Context de sa recherche
Harald Szeemann (1933–2005) was one of the most influential curators of the 20th century, known for revolutionizing the curator's role through a deeply creative, artist-collaborative approach. In 1974 he moved to Ticino, where he built his studio and archive in Maggia. There he became fascinated by Monte Verità — a site near Ascona that had hosted artists, intellectuals, utopians and anarchists (Hugo Ball, Kafka, Jung, Hesse, Klee...) since the early 1900s.
In 1978 he curated Monte Verità. Le mammelle della verità across five venues in Ascona, later touring to Zurich, Berlin, Vienna and Munich. A permanent reduced version has been on display at Casa Anatta since 1981, reinstalled in 2017.
The archive is split between the Getty Research Institute (purchased in 2011, partially digitized) and Ticino — the Monte Verità Foundation and the State Archive in Bellinzona manage the local portion.

Project Goals
The 50th anniversary of the exhibition in 2028 is the occasion for:

A panoramic review and deep analysis of the archive documents (written and iconographic) held in Bellinzona and Casa Anatta
Digitization of a curated selection of documents, published under open licenses (CC0 for data, CC BY 4.0 for texts)
An online platform making the archive internationally accessible, built on existing systems (Sàmara) in collaboration with SUPSI's design institute
A popular illustrated publication for 2028, written in accessible language for a general audience


Research Focus
The project centers on the curatorial process: how Szeemann conceived and built the exhibition between 1974 and 1978 — from the first napkin sketches to the press reviews. Key themes include his collaborative network (the AGG — Agentur für Geistige Gastarbeit), his "museum of obsessions" approach, the exhibition as total artwork, utopian communities, outsider art, and the relationship between humans and nature.

Partners

SUPSI / Istituto design — research host institution, supervisor: Iolanda Pensa
Fondazione Monte Verità — initiator, involved throughout (Nicoletta Mongini)
Archivio di Stato del Cantone Ticino, Bellinzona — archive access and specialist support
Getty Research Institute — consultation on shared documentation (Pietro Rigolo)


Timeline (24 months, 6 four-month phases)
The project moves from initial archive exploration and concept definition → document selection and digitization → platform implementation → final text editing and publication structure → delivery of the online platform and final report.

Key Outputs

Online archive platform (open access, globally accessible)
Digitized documents with descriptive texts
Structure and content for a 2028 illustrated publication
All data released under open licenses

### Le projet : Reconstitution de l'expo Monte Verità (1978)

Harald Szeemann avait conçu en 1978 une exposition autour de Monte Verità (Ascona) — la seule de ses expos qui subsiste encore sous forme réduite aujourd'hui. L'objectif est une **reconstitution pour le 50e anniversaire en 2028**.

**Structure de l'expo originale**
- ~1500 objets, 600 personnes, 600 « visions du paradis »
- Inventaire reconstitué à partir des listes d'assurance et de transport
- Expo itinérante (Italie, Autriche, Suisse alémanique)
- Catalogue académique séparé (≠ scénographie de l'expo)

**Ce que Jonas documente**
- Le processus curatorial de Szeemann
- Le réseau des années 70 : qui a mis en contact les 600 personnes avec Szeemann
- Le « méta-niveau » : documentation du réseau derrière le réseau
